About Taiwan

Location: Taiwan is an island located in East Asia, about 160 km off the southeastern coast of China. It is known for its diverse landscape, including mountains in the east and fertile plains in the west.

Government & Political Structure: Taiwan operates as a democratic republic with a president elected every four years. Its political system includes three branches: Executive, Legislative, and Judicial, with a strong focus on human rights and democratic values.

History: Taiwan’s history is rich and diverse, beginning with indigenous peoples and later influenced by Dutch, Qing, and Japanese rule. After World War II, Taiwan became the seat of the Republic of China (ROC), maintaining its status as a self-governing entity.

Economy: Taiwan is a global leader in the technology sector, particularly in semiconductor manufacturing. Its economy is export-driven, with electronics, machinery, and consumer goods being key exports. Taiwan’s economy is also strengthened by agriculture, including rice, tea, and tropical fruits.

Culture: Taiwan blends traditional Chinese culture with modern influences. Its festivals, temples, and food culture are renowned. The island's food scene is influenced by Chinese, indigenous, and international flavors, including iconic dishes like bubble tea and beef noodle soup.

Technology & Innovation: Taiwan is a global leader in technology and innovation, especially known for its semiconductor industry. Companies like Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) play a pivotal role in the global tech supply chain.

Tourism & Nature: Taiwan is known for its scenic beauty, from the lush mountains of Taroko Gorge to its picturesque beaches. Outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and visiting national parks make it a popular destination for travelers.
